Hello!

Hi Founders and Coders - thanks for taking the time to read through my application! I'm a 27-year-old from South London. After having spent 5 years working in B2B technology marketing, I'm looking for a change, and programming has been something I've been considering as an option for some time.

About Me

I graduated from Keele University with a degree in business and marketing in 2018, and found a job in B2B marketing immediately. While I enjoyed the work and working with a lovely team, it was clear that my personality is suited to technical problem-solving work. Throughout my spare time in my twenties I have had a strong interest in music technology and music production, so I took a career break in April 2022 to pursue becoming a full-time musician and producer.

Why software engineering?

During my time working as a musician over the past 18 months I have, once again, found myself gravitating towards the technical elements of the job; music mixing, along with experimenting with the creation of my own software instrument using Native Instruments' Kontakt KSP scripting language (https://www.native-instruments.com/fileadmin/ni_media/downloads/manuals/kontakt/KONTAKT_602_KSP_Reference_Manual.pdf). Using KSP I created my own software synth which I have recently released commercially, available here: https://batterseaaudio.co.uk/polygrade

What have I learnt so far?

Aside from my Kontakt scripting work, I have also spent time experimenting with C++, including doing an introductory course at Kadenze.com on audio plugin development using C++. As I continue with my first programming job I intend to continue developing audio plugins in my free time, in order to develop a portfolio.

I have really enjoyed working through the codewars challenges during my application, along with the java game and learning web development basics. I am excited to create a solid foundational understanding of HTML, Javascript, and CSS if I am accepted into the Founders & Coders course.
